SPATIO-TEMPORAL GRAPH-BASED SELF-LABELLING FOR
VIDEO ANOMALY DETECTION

Meng Xing1,3, Zhiyong Feng1, Yong Su2, Yiming Zhang1, Changjae Oh3

Tianjin University, China1
Tianjin Normal University, China2
Centre for Intelligent Sensing, Queen Mary University of London, United Kingdom3

ABSTRACT: Video anomaly detection (VAD) aims to identify abnormal events in a video sequence. Existing methods achieve VAD by learning the decision boundary between the normal space and the abnormal space pre-defined for training. However, these methods do not consider the gap between the pre-defined abnormal space and the real one. In this paper, we propose a novel self-labelling-based learning and combine this scheme with spatio-temporal graph convolutional network (ST-GCN) for VAD. ST-GCN is utilized to model the interaction between events between inter-frame objects and is trained with the proposed learning scheme. Our learning scheme allows the model to detect not-normal events instead of abnormal events, which shows a better generalization ability on unseen abnormal events. We validate our method on three datasets, showing state-of-the-art frame-level AUC results on Avenue (92.5%), competitive 96.5% AUC on UCSD Ped2 and 76.8% AUC on ShanghaiTech.



Firstly, the Object selection and ST-Graph Construction modules transform the input video sequence into the ST-Graph with the objects as nodes by utilizing the object detector and optical-flow extractor. By assuming that abnormal events tend to have larger motions than normal ones, we generate pseudo-abnormal events utilizing frames with larger time intervals. It is worth noting, the pseudo-anomalies assumption can be expanded into a more flexible assumption toolbox to address different scenarios. Secondly, the ST-Graph is fed into the ST-GCN to learn the spatial interaction of intra-frame objects and the temporal correlation of inter-frame objects. Finally, we train our model based on a new self-labelling-based learning scheme, which learns the decision boundary between normal and not-normal events.
